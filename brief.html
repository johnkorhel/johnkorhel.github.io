What is Augmented Reality?

Augmented reality is a piece of technology that turns the environment around the user into a digital interface. This is achieved by placing virtual objects in the real world, in real time. There are three main categories of AR tools.

AR Viewers - AR Viewers are simple, life size 3D models that users can make and view wherever they please. The creation of these 3D models can range from taking a panorama with a phone to sculpting a game in Unity.

AR Browsers - AR Browsers are simple and lightweight phone and tablet applications that absolutely transform the world around the user. Have you ever wondered how large the Empire State Building is? Just snap a picture with your phone and you’ll get the cost of construction, number of employees, and the exact height.

AR Gaming - The most prominent example of AR in our society is gaming. There are not too many games out as it stands, and AR is commonly mixed up with VR. The reason AR games are so hard to create is because developers need to base their design and levels around totally different environments depending on where the user is. One of the most prominent and expert uses of AR is in the popular game, Pokemon GO.

It is important to remember that AR and VR are two completely different things. The two both immerse the user, but AR keeps the user in touch with the real world, while VR puts the user into a completely virtual world.

Dangers of Augmented Reality

Distractions - In the world of technology there are always new and innovative tech that is coming out all the time. Along with these new technologies there come new dangers that can affect every person that uses the tech. With AR barely on the market there are still many studies that need to be done with it, however the information currently available we do know that Augmented Reality can cause heavy distractions. Just like texting on your phone you're paying more attention to the device and what it shows you rather than the world around you. Augmented Reality can cause you to become distracted with the text on the screen or the images that pop up making you pay less attention to your surroundings potentially putting you in danger. Imagine a scenario where you’re walking down the street and you get a funny meme from your friend, you open up the image and it fills the screen (google glasses) as you’re reading text on the picture you accidentally walk into the street where a car is coming.

Distractions are a real problem, for fighter pilots they have minimal displays with just information they need to know and no images that could distract them. Pilots also go through extensive training to be able to understand this information quickly and pay attention to their surroundings. On in informal study several hundred people used google glasses to see how their attention and sight was impaired. It was discovered that 5 to 10 percent struggle while using glasses to focus on objects for 20 seconds before giving up. Then 25 percent of people had difficulty focusing but eventually were able to handle google glasses. This data shows that at least initially google glasses impairs your ability to focus on your surroundings. Focus is not the only problem however, with all the images and text the pops up on the screen your vision is obscured so that you might not notice as much in your surroundings like a car. This can happen when a notification pops up on the side of the screen. You might ignore it as you cross the street but the danger is still present because you are consciously aware of the notification and are probably thinking about it, this can cause you to be distracted and potentially cause you to hit something.

Vision - There are several things that you have to consider when using products like google glasses such as peripheral vision, central vision, and your focus. Peripheral vision is much more essential than you might think towards your ability to recognize what's happening around you. Peripheral vision gives you sensory information like rough estimates of how far objects are from you, estimate of movement around you. Interfering with peripheral vision can cause you to misjudge the distance of objects or the relative motion they have causing you to bump into objects or stumble. Central vision is key to knowing what's happening around you but peripheral vision is essential to knowing what is coming up.
